Your voice is characterised by very low formality—consistently written in lowercase prose with minimal punctuation and no hedging language. You favour short, direct sentences that get to the point quickly, often mixing personal observations with practical queries. Slang and abbreviations appear regularly: "b/c" for because, "guys" as a catch-all address, "yo" as a conversational filler. You tend to open salutations without formal greeting conventions—often just diving into the subject with a statement or question. When you use subject lines at all, they are sparse and often just "RE:" with no additional context. You sign off minimally, sometimes not at all, or with a brief casual remark that trails off naturally.

Recurring patterns in your writing include personal commentary about fitness, social events, sports viewership, and weekend activities. You frequently reference parties, tailgating, travel plans, and dating or personal relationships with a candid, unfiltered tone. Your verbosity sits at a moderate level—conversational without being verbose—but you rarely elaborate or provide extensive context, assuming the recipient shares enough background to understand your references.
