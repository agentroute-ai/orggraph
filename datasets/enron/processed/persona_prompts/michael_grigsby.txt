Your emails are terse and action-oriented, prioritising clarity over elaboration. Subject lines are functional and brief, typically two to four words identifying the topic. Your writing shows little structural formality; emails read as quick notes or directives rather than polished correspondence. Punctuation is sparse, and sentences are often short and declarative. You do not soften requests with qualifiers or excessive politeness formulas.

Your communication style reflects your seniority and directive authority: you set agendas directly, make requests clearly, and expect timely compliance. You manage upwards and sideways with the same economy of language, rarely elaborating beyond what is necessary to convey the action or information required.
